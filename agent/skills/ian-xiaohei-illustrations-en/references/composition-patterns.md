# Composition Patterns and Originality Rules

## Basic Structure Types

Choose one structure only. Do not mix too many.

### Workflow

Best for input -> process -> output, content production, AI workflows, and automation chains.

Layout: input on the left, Xiaohei or a strange machine processing in the middle, output on the right, with orange arrows showing the main flow.

### System Fragment

Best for information sources, filters, databases, renderers, and agent subsystems.

Layout: draw only 3-5 core modules, with Xiaohei taking one key action.

### Before/After Contrast

Best for messy/ordered, manual/automatic, scattered/collected, anxious/stable.

Layout: messy left, stable right, orange arrow in the middle. Characters can be more exaggerated.

### Character State

Best for pain points, creator state, too many tools, information anxiety, and stuck -> moving.

Layout: 2-4 small states, each with a short label.

### Conceptual Metaphor

Best for content factories, information warehouses, brain black boxes, workflow machines, and auto reports.

Layout: one big strange object or machine, a few inputs, one output. It needs a memorable image.

### Layered Method

Best for frameworks, system layers, capability stacks, and content system layers.

Layout: stacked boxes, not a formal pyramid; Xiaohei can build nearby or move bricks.

### Route Map

Best for idea to launch, user path, content handoff path, and learning routes.

Layout: one curving path, a few nodes, Xiaohei walking or pulling a thread.

### Small Comic Sequence

Best for failure to success, real process, jokes, and before/after use cases.

Layout: 2-4 small scenes, each showing one action.

## Original Metaphor Method

Every time, reinvent the metaphor from the current article. Do not copy old images.

### Three Steps

1. Turn the abstract concept into a physical action: get stuck, leak, get heavier, sort, settle, ferment, open a door, fold, unpack, or recirculate.
2. Turn the system structure into a low-tech object: broken machine, cardboard box, drawer, pipe, mailbox, odd dial, scale, well, ladder, or strange workbench.
3. Make Xiaohei perform the action: not standing beside it, but stuck in the machine, pulling the wrong wire, guarding the gate, carrying, repairing, weighing, holding a ladder, recording, or stuffing something into a strange device.

### Object Pool

- Cardboard boxes, drawers, old machines, funnels, scales, mailboxes, doors, wells, ladders, pipes, string balls, gates, turntables, black boxes, punchers, noodle presses, clotheslines, and strange workstations.
- Pick only 1-2 at a time. Do not pile them up.

### Xiaohei Action Pool

- Pull, carry, stuff, scoop, press, weigh, sew, cut, twist, guard, push, catch, dismantle, mark, and recover.
- The action should serve the core idea. Do not be weird for its own sake.

## Repetition Rules

Do not open or reuse `assets/examples/` by default. These images are only for calibration, such as line density, whitespace, color restraint, and Xiaohei's presence.

Unless the user explicitly says "copy this image", "recreate this composition", or "use this case", do not directly reuse these old compositions:

- Two breakpoints on a conveyor belt
- Xiaohei pulling a decision lever inside a content machine
- Xiaohei as a funnel sorting traffic, trust, and conversion
- Xiaohei cutting a material fish
- Xiaohei pulling a handoff path
- Xiaohei pulling three layers of information sources
- Three Xiaoheis holding a megaphone, building a bridge, and opening a door
- Xiaohei stamping a phrase toolbox
- Xiaohei holding a sign for common-pitfall paths

Similar themes should still use a new metaphor. For example, "handoff path" does not have to be a route; Xiaohei could attach the tail of the content to a door handle. "One fish, many uses" does not have to be a fish; Xiaohei could press a paper ball into several shapes.
